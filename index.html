I am a Visiting Researcher at the Wharton School (University of Pennsylvania) and a graduate student in Finance and Information Management (FIM) at the Technical University of Munich. Before that, I studied Industrial Engineering and Management to pursue my interest in applied math and physics. I have also followed the startup ecosystem closely for many years — currently through the Manage and More scholarship at UnternehmerTUM. My research sits at the intersection of causal machine learning, human–AI collaboration, and agentic AI.

Research

My current work investigates how causal machine learning can be integrated into clinical decision support systems so that clinicians can reason about treatment effects, not just predictions. I design and evaluate information systems built on EHR data, study workflow integration through clinician-in-the-loop experiments, and care about what makes causal estimates trustworthy enough to act on in high-stakes settings.

In parallel, I work on agentic AI: how it is changing the way humans interact with the web, how such agents can be detected, and what infrastructure should look like for the agentic era ahead.
