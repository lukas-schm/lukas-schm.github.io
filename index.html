Visiting Researcher, The Wharton School · M.Sc. Candidate, TUM

I am a Visiting Researcher at the Wharton School (University of Pennsylvania) and an M.Sc. candidate in Finance and Information Management at the Technical University of Munich. My research sits at the intersection of causal machine learning and human–AI collaboration, with a focus on clinical decision support — using EHR data to estimate individual treatment effects and enable counterfactual reasoning at the point of care.

Research

My current work investigates how causal machine learning can be integrated into clinical decision support systems so that clinicians can reason about treatment effects, not just predictions. I design and evaluate prototype systems on EHR data, study workflow integration through clinician-in-the-loop experiments, and care about what makes causal estimates trustworthy enough to act on in high-stakes settings.
